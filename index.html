How to Vote Step by Step

Follow these 5 simple steps to exercise your democratic right

1
Check Your Eligibility

You must be a citizen of India, at least 18 years old, and ordinarily resident in the constituency where you want to vote.

2
Register on the Electoral Roll

Fill Form 6 online at voters.eci.gov.in or visit your local BLO (Booth Level Officer). Provide Aadhaar, photo ID, and address proof.

3
Receive Your EPIC Card

After verification, you'll receive your Electors' Photo Identity Card (EPIC / Voter ID). It typically takes 4–6 weeks. Download a digital copy from the portal.

4
Find Your Polling Booth

Use our Booth Locator tab or visit the ECI portal to find your assigned polling station. Note the date and timings (usually 7 AM – 6 PM).

5
Cast Your Vote

Carry a valid photo ID. The EVM (Electronic Voting Machine) will display candidates. Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate. VVPAT confirms your vote.

Your Voter Rights

The Constitution of India guarantees these rights to every eligible voter

Secret Ballot

Your vote is completely private. No one—including election officials—can see whom you voted for.

Paid Leave to Vote

Under the Representation of the People Act, your employer must grant you paid leave on election day.

Challenge Your Entry

If someone else tries to impersonate you at the booth, you have the right to challenge the officer and cast a Tendered Vote.

Right to NOTA

"None of the Above" is a valid option on every EVM. If you are unsatisfied with all candidates, you can use NOTA without nullifying your vote.

Accessibility Support

Senior citizens (85+) and persons with disabilities can vote from home using Postal Ballot or get special assistance at the polling booth.

Right to Know

Under the SC ruling (2003), voters have the right to know the criminal records, assets, and educational qualifications of every candidate.
